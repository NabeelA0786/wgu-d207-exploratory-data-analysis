Continuous Dependent Variable:
>T-test - Tests 1-2 groups
>ANOVA - Tests more than two groups

Categorical Dependent Variable:
>Chi Square

Data Prep:
-Talk about removing blanks, NAs
-Remove categoricals with high cardinality > 3 to 5 levels
-Consider removing outliers > 3 standard deviations
-Remember to convert chr to str data to categoricals
-Once you convert to categorical then convert to numeric levels. ex. small - 0, medium - 1, large - 2 (Not too important in this course)
-Do not OVERTHINK

Univariate v. Bivariate graphs:
-Univariate means 'one variable'. A graph of a continuous variable like a histogram. A graph of a categorical variable like a bar graph. Provide a table of values below to improve understanding.
-Bivariate graphs use same datatype: cont v. cont; cat v. cat
-Scale and title the x-y axis, main title for clarity
-Explain graph to readers for better understanding
-Converting categorical data to numeric can improve graphics
-Stay away from multidimensional graphs cause meaning and interpretation can be lost
